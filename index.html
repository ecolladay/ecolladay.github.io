Welcome

Hi there, my name's Emily

I am a Computer Science Master's student studying at Northeastern University in Boston, Massachusetts. Though my background is in International Affairs, I have found myself on this path in an effort to find quantifiable explanations and solutions for the concepts we study in the social sciences. At times hectic and complex, is it the wealth of possibility that drives me forward in this technical field.
Currently, I am seeking an internship to further develop my skillset in this exciting industry.
On this website, I have summarized my past projects for you to browse through (and for myself as motivation in those dark, likely seg fault-plagued times). Feel free to say hello via email, or read more about how I found myself here!

Projects

Shapes Animator

Diving into Object-Oriented Design and Java and Java Swing, I created a project using Model-View-Controller design that takes in a text file of instructions to create an animation of given shapes, with play, pause, rewind and speed features.

UNFPA Global Learning Map

After my internship with the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA), I reached out to initiate an interactive global map that visualizes their 2019 UNFPA initiatives and recorded country-level data. Using JavaScript, CSS, and HTML, my group and I created an online tool, hosted on Github Pages, that was then shown during a quarterly UN meeting for further development.

Analysis of Violent Crime in the U.S. and Mexico

Using classic statistical Python libraries such as NumPy and scikit-learn on governmental data of both the U.S. and Mexico, this project seeks a look into the distribution of violent crimes, disaggregated by various categories such as year, sex of offender, and weapon used.

Refugees and Germany: The Discrepancy in Data and Opinion

During my internship with the Institute for Economics and Peace, I worked on the first German Peace Index, conducting research in German and English. This article displays much of my analysis of the statistics on refugees and crime during the height of the refugee crisis in Europe, as public opinion grew increasingly negative despite a more nuanced story derived from the data.

  • More

Failing Justice: Mexico’s Institutional Weaknesses

During my internship with the Institute for Economics and Peace, I wrote an article on the research I had conducted on attitudinal data of Mexican mindset of their safety and the institutions sworn to protect them. This, coupled with rates of crime, impunity, and militarisation paint a clear picture of institutional weakness and its detrimental effect on a population.
