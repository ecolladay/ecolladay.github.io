Analysis of Violent Crime in the U.S. and Mexico

Class: Programming with Data
Language: Python
Tools & Libraries: NumPy, matplotlib, pandas, scikit-learn, Jupyter Notebook

Short: Using classic statistical Python libraries such as NumPy and scikit-learn on governmental data of both the U.S. and Mexico, this project seeks a look into the distribution of violent crimes, disaggregated by various categories such as year, sex of offender, and weapon used.

Data Sourcing

As I had worked on Mexican crime data druing my internship with the Institute for Economics and Peace, I was familiar with online resources and naming conventions in Spanish translated to English. Mexico has been investing in better quality data by recording more details and providing all of it online via the statistical governmental institution, the Secretariado Ejecutivo del Sistema Nacional de Seguridad PÃºblica (SESNSP). This resource can be found here. These databases offer records dating back to 1997, with various disaggregations and filters to be applied, such as municipal or state-level data, monthly or annual data, as well as demographic information. All these details can paint a more precise picture of crime statistics in Mexico - an important step toward tackling the issues that disrupt society. For our purpose, we decided to focus only on certain violent crimes and categories, choosing those that we also found good data for in American databases for American crime.

I was less familiar with online resources for American data, yet there was enough available to draw comparisons to Mexican statistics - given the caveat of different recording practices and underreported cases. As this analysis would remain at the big-picture level, this was not a concern. Finding one good database was not possible, as much was divided by states that reported their own statistics. While Mexican states also report their own numbers, SESNSP provides aggregated datasets. Despite this initial obstacle, our team pulled from various sources, such as Data.gov, to compile our own database.
